Import Guide

What is import?

Import is the process of bringing goods or services into a country from abroad.

Who can engage in imports?

Importing is generally the prerogative of businesses and individuals involved in trade. To engage in imports, you may need specific permissions and licenses.

What should you do first when starting imports?

Before starting imports, it is crucial to understand customs and trade regulations, obtain necessary permits, establish communication with suppliers, and determine payment methods.

Can you import any item you want? What will you do if you want to see a sample of the product you want to import?

There may be restrictions on the type of product you want to import, depending on the country. To request a sample, you can communicate with your supplier.

From which country will you import?

Different countries may be preferred depending on the products and services you want to import. Conducting market research before importing is essential.

What documents will customs authorities request from you during import procedures?

Customs procedures usually require documents such as an invoice, transportation document, and certificate of origin. Providing these documents accurately and promptly is important.

What kind of checks are conducted during imports?

Customs authorities may conduct various checks during the import process based on the type and characteristics of the goods. Checks may include aspects such as health, safety, and compliance with standards.

How much tax will you pay for the goods you import?

The amount of tax can vary based on the type and value of the imported goods and the country. To learn and pay tax rates, it is essential to communicate with customs authorities.

What do you need to do before starting customs procedures in imports?

Before starting customs procedures, understanding customs regulations and requirements, preparing necessary documents, and obtaining appropriate permits are important steps.

When should you perform customs procedures for the goods you will import?

Customs procedures should be completed either before or immediately after the goods enter the country. The process and requirements may vary depending on the country.

How can you conduct customs procedures in imports?

Customs procedures are usually carried out through a customs broker or by directly communicating with the customs administration.

How do you pay the taxes arising from customs procedures in imports?

Import taxes are typically paid by the importer or customs broker. Tax payment processes may vary depending on the country.

What should you do after completing your import?

After completing an import, it is important to update inventory management, organize accounting records, and initiate sales processes as needed.

What can you do if you want to return the product you imported?

If you want to return an imported product, you should learn about the return policies, communicate with the supplier, and adhere to the necessary procedures.

How do you pay the cost of the imported item to the buyer?

The cost of the imported item is usually paid to the buyer according to predetermined payment terms, through methods such as wire transfer or other payment methods.

Can commercial items be brought by mail?

Commercial items are generally allowed to be brought by mail, but there may be restrictions depending on the country. It is important to carefully review customs regulations.

If items brought with the passenger are considered commercially, what procedures are followed?

If items brought with a passenger are considered commercial, customs procedures and taxation are conducted according to commercial import rules.

What are the differences between paid import and duty-free import?

Paid import refers to imports made for a specific fee, while duty-free import is usually conducted for tax exemption or assistance purposes.

Some warnings and advice for our importers

Individuals engaging in imports are advised to closely follow customs regulations, choose reliable suppliers, carefully examine payment terms, and comply with local legal regulations.
