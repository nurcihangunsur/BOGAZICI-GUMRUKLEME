External Processing Guide

What is the Outward Processing Regime?

The Outward Processing Regime is a customs regime that allows goods entering the country for a specific process to be re-exported after undergoing that process.

What are the objectives of the Outward Processing Regime?

The objectives of the Outward Processing Regime include the development of foreign trade, support for local industries, and gaining a competitive advantage.

What are the conditions for benefiting from the Outward Processing Regime?

To benefit from the Outward Processing Regime, specific conditions must be met. These conditions are subject to customs regulations and regime requirements.

Are there goods that are not covered by the Outward Processing Regime?

Yes, certain goods may not be covered by the Outward Processing Regime. This depends on customs regulations and regime conditions.

Who is granted permission for the Outward Processing Regime?

Permission for the Outward Processing Regime is granted to individuals or companies authorized by the customs administration.

Where can you obtain permission for the Outward Processing Regime?

Permission for the Outward Processing Regime can be obtained by applying to the customs administration. The application process is subject to specific procedures.

Is there a time limit in the Outward Processing Regime?

Yes, the Outward Processing Regime has a specific time limit. The processed goods must be exported within a certain period.

Is it possible to revise Outward Processing permits?

The possibility of revising Outward Processing permits may be subject to specific conditions according to customs regulations. Generally, there may be an option to revise in certain situations.

How is taxation handled in the Outward Processing Regime?

In the Outward Processing Regime, taxation occurs not at the importation stage of the processed goods but at the exportation stage. Therefore, taxes are applied when the processed goods are exported.

What is the Standard Exchange System?

The Standard Exchange System is an international system used in foreign trade that ensures the mutual convertibility of trade units valid in different countries.

How does the Outward Processing Regime come to an end?

The Outward Processing Regime comes to an end when the processed goods are exported within a certain period. Additionally, the regime must be terminated in accordance with customs regulations.

What happens in case of non-compliance with Outward Processing measures?

In case of non-compliance with Outward Processing measures, sanctions determined by the customs administration may be applied. These sanctions may vary depending on the nature of the violation.
