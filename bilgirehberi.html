Customs and Trade Guide

What is Duty-Free Export?

Duty-free export is a type of export where no payment is received for the goods, usually conducted for special circumstances or aid purposes.

What does Declaration Control mean?

Declaration control is the examination by customs authorities to verify the accuracy and appropriateness of the customs declaration. It is a crucial stage in the customs process.

What does "Intellectual and Industrial Property Rights" mean?

Intellectual and industrial property rights refer to the rights a person holds over products resulting from creative or commercial activities. This includes rights such as patents, trademarks, and copyrights.

What is Temporary Export?

Temporary export is the practice of sending goods out of a country for a specific period but returning them in the same form or with modifications later.

What is Temporary Import?

Temporary import is the practice of bringing goods into a foreign country for a specific period but sending them back in the same form or with modifications later.

What is Returned Goods?

Returned goods are items or products that were previously exported but are brought back within a specific period. This often involves temporary export and import processes.

Who is a Customs Consultant?

A customs consultant is a professional specialized in customs procedures and regulations. They represent companies or individuals in customs procedures and provide consultancy services.

What are Customs Approved Operation or Use and Customs Regimes?

Customs Approved Operation or Use is a customs regime that grants authorization to businesses with a specific status to import or export within a certain period.

What is Service Export?

Service export refers to the services provided by a resident firm or individual to a customer in another country. It emphasizes service trade rather than goods trade.

About Exporters' Associations

Exporters' associations are trade organizations that focus on similar industries or sectors, promoting collaboration among member firms. They work to protect the interests of exporters.

What are Lists Attached to Import Regime Decision?

Lists Attached to Import Regime Decision are official documents specifying the tax rates, quotas, and other regulations applicable to the import of a particular product.

What is Refund of Dues?

Refund of dues is a request for the full or partial refund of customs duties paid on a previous date for a particular item or goods.

What does Final Use mean?

Final use refers to the import of goods for a specific purpose, with approval from customs authorities for the intended use.

What is a Proforma Invoice?

A proforma invoice is a special type of invoice used in international trade that includes the price, quantity, and other essential information about a product.

What is Summary Declaration?

Summary declaration is a short and concise declaration form used to expedite customs procedures, representing import or export.

What is a Free Zone?

A free zone is a specific geographic area where trade of goods and services occurs without customs duties and other trade barriers.

What is Border Trade?

Border trade refers to trade activities that take place in border areas between two countries. It is often subject to specific regulations.

What is Liquidation?

Liquidation is the process of selling a company's assets and using the proceeds to pay off its debts. In the customs context, it may involve paying taxes or customs fees on goods.

What is Single Window System?

Single Window System is a system where trade transactions are conducted through a single platform. It aims to simplify processes and reduce bureaucratic procedures in trade.

What does "Preferential Trade" mean?

Preferential trade relies on the trade advantages recognized by two or more countries in their trade, often involving trade agreements with customs duty reductions or exemptions.

What does the "Free Circulation of Goods" principle, one of the fundamental principles of Customs Union, mean?

In Customs Union, the principle of the free circulation of goods means allowing a good legally sold in one member country to circulate freely in other member countries without hindrance. It aims to liberalize trade and unify the internal market.

Payment Methods in International Trade

Various payment methods exist in international trade:
  • Open Account: The seller receives payment within a specified period after delivering the goods.
  • Letter of Credit (L/C): The buyer provides a bank guarantee for payment; the seller receives payment by presenting specific documents.
  • Letter of Guarantee: The bank provides a letter of guarantee that the buyer will make the payment.
  • Cash in Advance: The buyer makes payment before the goods are shipped.

Delivery Terms in International Trade

Common delivery terms in international trade include:
  • EXW (Ex Works): The seller delivers the goods at the production site or warehouse.
  • FOB (Free on Board): The seller delivers the goods to a specified port; transportation costs are the buyer's responsibility.
  • CIF (Cost, Insurance and Freight): The seller delivers the goods to a specified port with transportation, insurance, and freight costs included.
  • DDP (Delivered Duty Paid): The seller delivers the goods to a specified location, including customs duties.

What is the Obligee Record and Tracking System?

The Obligee Record and Tracking System is a system where documents related to customs procedures and trade activities are recorded and tracked electronically. This system helps manage transactions more transparently and efficiently.
