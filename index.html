Frequently Asked Questions

What documents are required for customs clearance?

Documents such as invoice, transportation document, packing list are usually required for customs clearance

How are customs duties calculated?

Customs duties are usually calculated on the import value. The tax rate is determined using the customs tariff schedule.

How to fill out a customs declaration?

A customs declaration is an official document submitted to the customs authority for import or export transactions. It usually contains information such as product description, value, quantity and country of origin.

What is the difference between export and import processes?

Exporting is the process of sending goods or services from one country to another. Imports are the purchase of goods or services from one country to another. The main difference between the two processes is the direction of the goods or services.

What is customs warehousing and customs exemption?

Customs warehousing is the process of storing goods without paying customs duties. Customs exemption is the exemption of goods that meet certain conditions from customs duties.

How can I follow the changes in customs legislation?

It is important to regularly check the official website of the customs authority and current announcements to keep abreast of changes in customs legislation.

What should I pay attention to during customs inspections?

During customs inspections, it is important to provide accurate and complete documentation, comply with customs administration instructions and be descriptive. In addition, the goods or services subject to inspection must comply with the customs declaration.

Why is it important to be compliant with customs legislation?

Compliance with customs legislation is important to comply with legal requirements and avoid possible penalties. It is also necessary to carry out transactions in a faster and more organized manner.

What are special customs regimes?

Special customs regimes are special arrangements that provide exemption or reduction of customs duties under certain conditions. Examples include the free circulation regime or customs union agreements.

How should a customs broker be selected?

When choosing a customs broker, it is important to consider factors such as experience, areas of expertise, references and communication skills. In addition, the service fee offered by the customs broker should also be taken into account.
