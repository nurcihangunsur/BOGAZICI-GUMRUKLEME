Origin Guide

Why is the origin of goods important?

The origin of goods is important in determining customs duties, benefiting from trade agreements, and implementing other trade policies.

What does "Origin of Goods" mean?

"Origin of Goods" is a concept that indicates in which country a product was produced.

How is the origin of goods determined?

The origin of goods is generally determined based on the processes carried out where the goods are produced and the materials used.

What does "Preference Rules of Origin" mean?

Preference Rules of Origin define customs duty reductions or exemptions applied to products that meet certain conditions.

What does "Non-Preference Rules of Origin" mean?

Non-Preference Rules of Origin state that products not meeting specific conditions are subject to standard customs duties.

How is the origin of goods documented?

The origin of goods is typically documented with proof of origin documents. These documents may include invoices, certificates of origin, or similar documents.

What is the difference between acquiring the origin of goods and entering into free circulation?

Acquiring the origin of goods means meeting specific rules of origin, while entering into free circulation means being able to circulate freely without customs procedures.

Why are proof of origin or circulation documents issued in international trade transactions?

Proof of origin or circulation documents are issued to benefit from preferential customs duty rates or to ensure compliance with trade policies.

What are proof of origin and circulation documents? In which cases are they issued?

Proof of origin documents may include a Certificate of Origin. These documents are used to verify in which country a specific product was produced and can be issued according to trade agreements.

How are proof of origin and circulation documents issued?

Proof of origin and circulation documents are usually issued in accordance with rules determined by the relevant customs authority in the country where the goods are produced.

A reminder regarding proof of origin and circulation documents

When issuing proof of origin and circulation documents, attention should be paid to specific rules and requirements, ensuring their accuracy.

About checks on proof of origin and circulation documents

Proof of origin and circulation documents may be examined in checks conducted by customs authorities. Various sanctions may be applied if the documents are incomplete or incorrect.
