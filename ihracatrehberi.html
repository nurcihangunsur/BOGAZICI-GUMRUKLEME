Export Guide

What is export?

Export is the sale of goods or services from one country to another.

What are the advantages of exporting?

Exporting provides businesses with opportunities to enter new markets, create growth prospects, and gain a competitive advantage.

How can you become an exporter?

To engage in export, it is important to register with chambers of commerce, obtain necessary documents, and understand export regulations.

Can you export any goods you want?

When exporting, there may be restrictions depending on the type of product you intend to export and the regulations of the destination country.

What documents do you need for customs when exporting?

Typically, documents such as an invoice, transportation document, and certificate of origin are required for export transactions. It is crucial for these documents to be complete and accurate.

If you want to send a sample of the product you are exporting, what will you do?

If you want to send a sample of the product you are exporting, you can ship it through courier services in compliance with customs regulations.

What do you need to do before starting customs procedures in export?

Before starting customs procedures in export, it is important to understand customs regulations, prepare necessary documents, and obtain the required permits.

How can you complete customs procedures in export?

Customs procedures in export are usually carried out through a customs broker or by directly communicating with the customs administration.

When and how is your export completed?

The export process is completed when the products reach the destination country and are accepted by the buyer. The process may vary depending on the type of product sent and the destination country.

If the recipient or the official authorities of the importing country do not accept the product you exported and send it back to you, what should you do?

If the product you exported is sent back, you should check the status of the shipment and, if necessary, try to resolve issues by communicating with the buyer.

What does temporary export mean?

Temporary export is a trade method where goods are exported for a specific period and must be returned in the same condition.

What kind of facilitations are provided for inputs imported for the production of the product you will export?

Exporters may benefit from tax advantages or exemptions for the import of inputs used in production. This can vary depending on local regulations.

What should you do after completing your export?

After completing an export, it is important to update inventory management, organize accounting records, and evaluate customer satisfaction.

Important warnings and advice for our exporters

Individuals engaging in exports are advised to carefully follow the regulations of the destination country, choose reliable logistics partners, clearly define payment terms, and prioritize customer support services.
